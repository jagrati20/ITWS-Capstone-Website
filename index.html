About Immuno-Oncology Treatment

[Placeholder- Source:Facing Immunotherapy]​​
​The essential concept of immunotherapy is that an individual’s cancer can be eradicated if the immune system can be instructed to do so.​ The immune system serves as our body’s quality control and recycling system, inspecting and removing our own cells and tissues when they become damaged or diseased. In this way, our immune system protects us against cancer, by detecting and killing abnormal cells that could turn into cancer. Cancers have found ways to hide from the normal immune system. The goal of immunotherapy is to re-train and enable the immune system to better attack cancer that has managed to evade it.
[Placeholder- Source: Facing Immunotherapy]​
Immunotherapy works by helping the immune system overcome the obstacles to recognizing and destroying cancer cells. Immunotherapy can work by:
  • Boosting immune system activity.
  • Removing the brakes that prevent immune cell activation.
  • Allowing the immune system to recognize cancer cells more easily
[Placeholder- Source: Facing Immunotherapy]
​Immunotherapy has varying effects on cancers.  ​For some diseases, immunotherapy can dramatically reduce the cancer size in a significant percentage of individuals, prolong survival, and may even cure a fraction of them. In other cancers, immunotherapy can reduce the cancer size and prolong survival, but may not able to cure the cancer entirely. The effects of immunotherapy depend on many factors, including the specific treatment being used, the type of cancer, and your own immune system.
